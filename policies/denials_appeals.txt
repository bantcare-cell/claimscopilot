DENIALS AND APPEALS POLICY

Common denial reasons: Claims are most often denied for: late submission (past
90 days), lack of prior authorization for services that require it, service not
covered under the member's plan, duplicate submission, or provider not in network.

Appeals process: A member or provider may appeal a denial within 180 days of the
denial notice. First-level appeals are reviewed by a claims specialist within
30 days. If the first-level appeal is denied, a second-level appeal may be filed
within 60 days and is reviewed by a medical director.

Prior authorization: Services requiring prior authorization include: continuous
glucose monitors, insulin pumps, inpatient admissions (except emergencies), and
any single service billed above $5,000. Authorization requests are answered
within 5 business days.

Emergency exception: Emergency services are always covered at in-network rates,
even from out-of-network providers, and never require prior authorization.
